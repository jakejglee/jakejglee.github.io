bellsprout

After college, I wanted to do some small-scale farming to make a little bit of side money. In terms of $/space ratio, wasabi seemed like a good choice since it can fetch $100/lb and I know a few sushi chefs who were interested in buying local wasabi.

Two years passed and I moved away from agriculture and into tech while passively watering my gardening hobby in my back yard. One day at Yamagami's Nursery I saw some wasabi plants for sale and took it as a sign. I bought one, found one more for half the price at Summerwinds Nursery that same day, and planted my two plants in my shady yard February 2020. They stayed...alive at least for a while. Then everything changed when the Summer attacked.

Who would have guessed, but wasabi does not do well in warm climates. Faced with wither, certain plant death, and a yard full of junk and gardening supplies, bellsprout was born. It is a wicking bed built from an old cooler and some tubing that is a great size for two wasabi plants. This keeps the plants mobile so that they can be moved indoors during the hot summer day and back outside at night.

This project is meant to evolve into aeroponically growing wasabi, but in these challenging times I need to start by keeping my wasabi alive.
